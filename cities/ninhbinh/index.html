The Region

Karsts & Rice Paddies

Imagine Ha Long Bay's limestone karsts rising from emerald rice paddies instead of water—that's Ninh Binh. This UNESCO World Heritage landscape offers the same dramatic geology with a different, arguably more photogenic, backdrop.

Just two hours from Hanoi, Ninh Binh was Vietnam's ancient capital before Hanoi. Today, the region combines stunning natural scenery with ancient temples, pagodas, and some of the country's most iconic photo opportunities.

Explore

Must-See Sites

2-3 Hours

Tam Coc

Row through three caves on a sampan boat, surrounded by towering karsts and rice paddies. Most iconic Ninh Binh experience.

3-4 Hours

Trang An

UNESCO-listed boat journey through 9+ caves and past temples. Less crowded than Tam Coc with more cave exploration.

1-2 Hours

Mua Cave

Climb 500 steps to the dragon-adorned summit for the region's most spectacular panoramic views. Best at sunset.

More to See

Beyond the Boats

Bai Dinh Pagoda

Vietnam's largest pagoda complex with giant Buddha statues, 500 arhat statues, and a 100-ton bronze bell.

Hoa Lu Ancient Capital

10th-century temples marking Vietnam's first independent capital. Important historical site with beautiful architecture.

Cycling Tours

Rent a bike and explore quiet paths through rice paddies, past village homes, and beneath karst formations.

Cuc Phuong National Park

Vietnam's oldest national park with primate rescue center, ancient trees, and excellent hiking trails.

Plan Your Visit

Practical Info

Getting There

2 hours from Hanoi by bus, car, or motorbike. Trains to Ninh Binh station. Easy day trip or overnight.

Best Time

May-June for green rice paddies and golden harvest. September-October for ripe golden fields. Avoid rainy season.

How Long

Day trip covers highlights. 2 nights allows relaxed exploration of all major sites plus cycling.

Where to Stay

Tam Coc village has the best location. Ninh Binh city has more options but less atmosphere.
