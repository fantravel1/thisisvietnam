The City

Weekend Escape

Every weekend, Saigon empties as city dwellers flee to Vung Tau—a peninsular beach town where the Saigon River meets the sea. This is where Vietnamese come to swim, feast on seafood, and escape the urban chaos.

Known for its giant Christ statue (rivaling Rio's), French colonial lighthouse, and endless seafood restaurants, Vung Tau offers an authentic glimpse into Vietnamese beach culture. The hydrofoil ride from Saigon takes just 90 minutes.

Things to Do

Christ the King Statue

Climb 800+ steps to this 32-meter statue with arms outstretched. Stunning city and ocean views from the observation deck.

Back Beach

The main swimming beach with calm waters, seafood shacks, and beach chairs. Best in the morning before crowds arrive.

Lighthouse

The 1910 French lighthouse offers panoramic views. Visit at sunset for the best photography.

Seafood Feast

Streets lined with seafood restaurants—pick your fish from tanks, eat with local beer, and enjoy the seaside atmosphere.

White Palace

Former summer residence of Vietnamese emperors. Elegant French colonial architecture with sea views.

Con Dao Day Trip

Take a ferry or flight to the pristine Con Dao islands—Vietnam's best-kept natural paradise.
