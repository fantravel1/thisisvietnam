The City

Before the Crowds Come

Quy Nhon is what Da Nang was 15 years ago—stunning beaches, authentic local life, and almost no international tourists. This central coast city offers Vietnam's most beautiful coastline without the resort prices or crowds.

Once the heartland of the Champa Kingdom, the region is dotted with ancient brick towers. Today it's a working fishing city where locals still outnumber visitors, seafood is cheap and fresh, and beaches remain gloriously empty.

Explore

Things to Do

Ky Co Beach

Vietnam's most beautiful beach—crystal water, white sand, dramatic cliffs. Take a boat from the city for a day in paradise.

Eo Gio

Windswept cliff formations where waves crash spectacularly. Hike the coastal paths for stunning photography.

Cham Towers

Explore Banh It, Duong Long, and Twin Towers—brick temple ruins from the ancient Champa civilization.

Seafood Markets

Wake early for the fish market, then feast on the day's catch at beachside restaurants for a fraction of resort prices.

City Beach

The main beach curves along the city—join locals for dawn swims, evening strolls, and sunset seafood.

Hon Kho Island

Take a basket boat to this tiny island with pristine snorkeling, empty beaches, and fresh-grilled seafood.

Plan Your Visit

Practical Info

Getting There

Fly direct from Hanoi or Saigon (1 hour). Train along the scenic coastal route. Bus from Da Nang (5 hrs) or Nha Trang (4 hrs).

Best Time

March-September for best beach weather. Avoid October-December rainy season. January-February is cool but pleasant.

Getting Around

Rent a motorbike (essential for beaches). Grab works in the city. Boats to islands from the harbor.

How Long

3-5 days ideal. Full day for Ky Co/Eo Gio, day for Cham temples, days for beach relaxation.
