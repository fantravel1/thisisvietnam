The City

A City of Contrasts

Called Saigon by locals and visitors alike, Ho Chi Minh City is Vietnam's pulsating economic heart. Here, French colonial grandeur stands beside gleaming skyscrapers, ancient pagodas neighbor rooftop bars, and the past flows seamlessly into a dynamic future.

From the moment you arrive, you're swept into the legendary motorbike symphony—millions of two-wheelers flowing like rivers through streets that never truly sleep. This is a city of entrepreneurs and dreamers, where sidewalk cafes buzz with billion-dollar deals and street vendors have served the same recipes for generations.

Must See

Iconic Landmarks

Where history and modernity collide

Notre-Dame Cathedral

A stunning red-brick French colonial cathedral built in 1880, standing proudly in the heart of District 1. Its twin bell towers reach 58 meters into the Saigon sky.

French Colonial

Ben Thanh Market

The symbol of Saigon since 1912. This bustling market offers everything from silk and lacquerware to the freshest Vietnamese street food under one iconic roof.

Cultural Icon

Independence Palace

Where the Vietnam War ended. This preserved time capsule of 1960s architecture witnessed the historic moment when tanks rolled through its gates on April 30, 1975.

Historic

Bitexco Financial Tower

The 262-meter lotus bud-shaped skyscraper defines modern Saigon's skyline. The Saigon Skydeck on the 49th floor offers breathtaking 360-degree city views.

Modern Icon
Neighborhoods

Explore the Districts

Each district has its own distinct character

District 1

The beating heart of Saigon. Colonial landmarks, rooftop bars, luxury hotels, and Nguyen Hue walking street converge in this vibrant center.

City Center

Pham Ngu Lao

The legendary backpacker district. Budget hotels, travel agencies, street food stalls, and bars that never close line these famous streets.

Backpacker Hub

Cholon (District 5)

Saigon's Chinatown is a world unto itself. Ancient temples, traditional medicine shops, and the best dim sum this side of Hong Kong.

Chinatown

District 2 (Thao Dien)

The expat enclave across the river. Leafy villas, artisan cafes, international restaurants, and a more relaxed pace of life.

Expat Haven
History

War Remnants

Understanding Vietnam's pivotal chapter

Cu Chi Tunnels

An incredible underground network stretching over 250 kilometers, where Viet Cong guerrillas lived, fought, and survived American bombing campaigns.

Today you can crawl through widened sections of these tunnels, understanding how this remarkable system included hospitals, command centers, living quarters, and weapon workshops—all hidden beneath the jungle floor.

War Remnants Museum

One of the most visited museums in Vietnam, presenting a Vietnamese perspective on the "American War" through powerful photographs, military equipment, and personal accounts.

Flavors

Saigon Street Food

The city eats on the street

Banh Mi

Saigon's legendary sandwich—crispy baguette stuffed with pate, cold cuts, pickled vegetables, cilantro, and chili.

Com Tam

Broken rice with grilled pork chop, shredded pork skin, steamed egg—Saigon's beloved comfort food.

Goi Cuon

Fresh spring rolls with shrimp, pork, herbs, and vermicelli wrapped in translucent rice paper.

Ca Phe Sua Da

Vietnamese iced coffee with condensed milk—fuel for millions of Saigon motorbike riders daily.

Hu Tieu

Southern-style noodle soup with pork, shrimp, and a clear sweet broth—breakfast of champions.

Che

Sweet dessert soup with beans, jellies, fruits, and coconut milk—refreshing in Saigon's heat.

Plan Your Trip

Practical Information

Best Time to Visit

December to April (dry season). Expect heat year-round with temperatures averaging 28-35°C. Afternoon thunderstorms are common May-November.

Getting Around

Grab (ride-hailing app) is essential. Motorbike taxis are fastest for short trips. Walking District 1 is pleasant, but be prepared to cross flowing traffic!

Where to Stay

District 1 for convenience. Pham Ngu Lao for budget options. Thao Dien (D2) for a quieter vibe. Book early during Tet (Vietnamese New Year).

Money Tips

ATMs everywhere. Cash preferred for street food and markets. Credit cards accepted in malls and hotels. ~25,000 VND = $1 USD (approximate).
