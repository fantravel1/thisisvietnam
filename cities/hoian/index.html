The Town

Timeless Enchantment

Once Southeast Asia's greatest trading port, Hoi An has preserved centuries of merchant history in its wooden shop-houses, assembly halls, and temples. Japanese, Chinese, and European influences blend into something uniquely Vietnamese.

By day, wander through mustard-yellow buildings draped with bougainvillea. By night, the town transforms as thousands of silk lanterns cast their warm glow over the Thu Bon River. This is Vietnam at its most romantic.

Must See

Ancient Landmarks

Centuries of history preserved in wood and stone

Japanese Covered Bridge

Built by Japanese merchants in the 1590s to connect them with the Chinese quarter. The pagoda-topped bridge features a small temple and intricate carvings of dogs and monkeys—the years construction began and ended.

Icon of Hoi An

Chinese Assembly Halls

Five elaborately decorated halls built by Chinese merchant communities from Fujian, Cantonese, Hainan, Chaozhou, and other regions. Each is a masterpiece of Chinese temple architecture.

Cultural Treasures

Ancient Merchant Houses

Explore 200-year-old homes like Tan Ky House, where Chinese and Japanese architectural elements merge with Vietnamese design. Families still live in these heritage properties.

Living History

Quan Cong Temple

Dedicated to the Chinese God of War, this 17th-century temple features stunning carp-shaped rain spouts and a serene courtyard with a bonsai garden and incense-filled air.

Sacred Space
Monthly Magic

Full Moon Festival

When Hoi An glows brightest

On the 14th day of each lunar month, Hoi An turns off its electric lights and transforms into a dreamscape of silk lanterns, candle-lit streets, and floating offerings on the Thu Bon River.

The ancient town becomes pedestrian-only as traditional music fills the air, vendors sell handmade lanterns, and locals and visitors alike release paper lanterns and flower offerings onto the water, carrying wishes downstream.

Made to Measure

World-Famous Tailors

Custom fashion in 24 hours

Hoi An's tailoring tradition dates back centuries when merchants from across Asia came for custom silks. Today, over 500 tailor shops can copy any design—from couture gowns to bespoke suits—in as little as 24 hours.

Tailoring Tips

  • Bring photos or magazine clippings of desired styles
  • Allow time for at least two fittings
  • Feel the fabric quality before committing
  • Negotiate, but remember quality costs
  • Check reviews for reputable shops

Hoi An Cuisine

Dishes found nowhere else in Vietnam

Cao Lau

Hoi An's signature dish can only be made here—thick rice noodles in a small amount of broth with pork, greens, and crispy croutons. Legend says authentic cao lau noodles must use water from the ancient Ba Le well.

Must Try

White Rose

Delicate shrimp dumplings in translucent rice paper, shaped like roses. Made by only one family in Hoi An who supplies the entire town.

Banh Mi Phuong

Anthony Bourdain called it "a symphony in a sandwich." The legendary Banh Mi Phuong sets the standard for this Vietnamese classic.

Com Ga Hoi An

Chicken rice Hoi An style—turmeric-yellow rice cooked in chicken stock, topped with shredded poultry and herbs.

Fried Wonton

Crispy wontons topped with shrimp, pork, tomato, and fresh herbs—a uniquely Hoi An creation.

Beyond the Town

Beach & Countryside

There's more to Hoi An than the ancient town

An Bang Beach

Just 4km from town, this palm-fringed beach offers golden sand, excellent swimming, and beachfront restaurants perfect for sunset cocktails.

Beach Life

Tra Que Village

A 500-year-old farming village where organic herbs grow using seaweed fertilizer. Join cooking classes and farm tours.

Farm Fresh

Cham Islands

A UNESCO Biosphere Reserve just 18km offshore. Snorkel coral reefs, dive historic shipwrecks, or simply relax on pristine beaches.

Island Escape

Coconut Basket Boats

Navigate the coconut palm waterways in traditional round basket boats. Fishermen will spin you in circles while singing folk songs.

Unique Experience
Plan Your Trip

Practical Information

Best Time to Visit

February to April for dry weather. Time your visit for the full moon (14th lunar day) for the lantern festival. Avoid October-November (flooding season).

Getting There

Fly into Da Nang (DAD), then 30 minutes by taxi or Grab. Direct shuttles run regularly. The scenic train from Hanoi or Saigon stops at Da Nang.

Getting Around

The ancient town is best explored on foot (cars are banned). Rent a bicycle for beaches and countryside. Grab works well for longer distances.

Old Town Ticket

VND 120,000 for a pass granting access to 5 of 22 heritage sites. Valid for 24 hours. Includes museums, old houses, assembly halls, and the Japanese Bridge.
