The City

Vietnam's Most Livable City

Da Nang has transformed from a sleepy port town into Vietnam's showcase of urban planning and modern ambition. Clean streets, stunning bridges, world-class beaches, and a forward-thinking spirit make this Central Vietnam's crown jewel.

Nestled between the Hai Van Pass and the ancient town of Hoi An, Da Nang is the perfect base for exploring Central Vietnam. By day, surf pristine waves at My Khe Beach. By night, watch the Dragon Bridge breathe fire over the Han River as the city lights up.

Must See

Iconic Landmarks

From fire-breathing dragons to golden hands in the clouds

Dragon Bridge

The world's largest dragon-shaped bridge spans 666 meters across the Han River. Every weekend at 9 PM, this steel dragon comes alive—breathing fire and water to the delight of thousands gathered below.

Engineering Marvel

Golden Bridge

Held aloft by giant stone hands emerging from the mountainside, this 150-meter pedestrian bridge at Ba Na Hills has become one of the world's most photographed attractions since its 2018 debut.

Instagram Famous

Marble Mountains

Five limestone and marble hills named after the five elements—Metal, Wood, Water, Fire, and Earth. Climb through sacred caves, explore Buddhist sanctuaries, and be rewarded with panoramic views.

Sacred Sites

Lady Buddha

At 67 meters tall, this white marble statue of Guanyin at Linh Ung Pagoda is Vietnam's tallest. She stands on Son Tra Peninsula, watching over fishermen and the city below.

Spiritual Icon
Sun & Surf

World-Class Beaches

30 kilometers of pristine coastline

My Khe Beach

Named one of the world's most attractive beaches by Forbes, My Khe offers powdery white sand, gentle waves perfect for swimming, and a stunning backdrop of mountains.

During the Vietnam War, American soldiers called it "China Beach"—their R&R paradise. Today, it's a surfer's haven, a sunrise jogger's dream, and home to luxury resorts that line the coast.

Crystal Waters Surfing Sunrise Views Beach Clubs

Non Nuoc Beach

At the foot of the Marble Mountains, this quieter stretch offers luxury resorts and traditional fishing villages.

Peaceful Retreat

Bai Bac Beach

Hidden on Son Tra Peninsula, this secluded cove offers pristine swimming and snorkeling away from the crowds.

Hidden Gem

Lang Co Beach

Just over the Hai Van Pass, this lagoon-backed beach offers stunning views and fresh seafood straight from fishing boats.

Mountain Escape

Ba Na Hills

A French village in the clouds

Take the world's longest single-track cable car (5.8km) up to a fantastical French village perched 1,400 meters above sea level, where temperatures drop 10°C and reality bends.

Golden Bridge

Walk among the clouds on this architectural wonder held by giant stone hands

French Village

Cobblestone streets, a cathedral, and European architecture recreated in the mountains

Fantasy Park

One of Vietnam's largest indoor amusement parks with rides and games

Linh Ung Pagoda

A serene Buddhist temple with a 27-meter Shakyamuni Buddha statue

Central Vietnamese Cuisine

Where the food is bold, spicy, and unforgettable

Mi Quang

Da Nang's signature dish—turmeric-yellow noodles with shrimp, pork, herbs, and just a splash of rich broth.

Banh Xeo

Crispy sizzling crepes stuffed with shrimp, pork, and bean sprouts—Central Vietnam's version is smaller and crispier.

Banh Trang Cuon Thit Heo

DIY rolls with rice paper, boiled pork, fresh herbs, and a fermented shrimp dipping sauce.

Fresh Seafood

Pick your catch from tanks at beachside restaurants—grilled, steamed, or fried to perfection.

Plan Your Trip

Practical Information

Best Time to Visit

February to May for beach weather. Avoid October-November (typhoon season). The Dragon Bridge fires up year-round on weekends at 9 PM.

Getting There

Da Nang International Airport (DAD) has direct flights from across Asia. The train station connects to Hanoi and Saigon along the scenic coastal route.

Getting Around

Rent a motorbike to explore freely, or use Grab for convenience. The city is compact and many beaches are walkable from downtown.

Where to Stay

My Khe Beach for luxury resorts. An Thuong area for boutique hotels and nightlife. Son Tra for secluded beach resorts.
