The City

Gateway to Ha Long

Hai Phong is Vietnam's third-largest city and its most important port, yet it remains refreshingly off the tourist trail. Most visitors pass through en route to Cat Ba Island, missing a city rich in French colonial architecture, fiery cuisine, and genuine northern character.

Founded as a major port in 1888, Hai Phong played crucial roles in both French Indochina and the American War. Today it's a bustling industrial hub with tree-lined boulevards, ornate opera house, and some of the best seafood in Vietnam.

Things to Do

Cat Ba Island

Take the ferry to this stunning island—an alternative base for Ha Long Bay with national park hiking and rock climbing.

Do Son Beach

The closest beach to Hanoi, popular with locals. Visit the historic Do Son casino or join buffalo fighting festivals.

Colonial Architecture

Stroll past the grand Opera House, Du Hang Pagoda, and French-era buildings along the tree-lined boulevards.

Seafood Street

Head to the seafood restaurants along Le Chan district for the freshest catches prepared Hai Phong style.

Local Specialty

Banh Da Cua Hai Phong

Hai Phong's signature dish is bánh đa cua—a crab noodle soup made with distinctive red rice noodles. The broth simmers with crab, tomatoes, and shrimp paste, topped with crab meat, fried tofu, and morning glory.

This dish exemplifies Hai Phong's bold, savory flavors that distinguish northern coastal cuisine. Try it at local shops near the markets for the most authentic experience.
