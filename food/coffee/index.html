The Culture

Slow Drip

Vietnamese coffee is strong, sweet, and meant to be savored slowly. The French introduced coffee here, but Vietnam made it uniquely its own with the phin filter and condensed milk.

Vietnam is the world's second-largest coffee producer, growing mostly robusta beans in the Central Highlands. The result is a bold, almost chocolatey coffee that's perfect with sweetened condensed milk.

Coffee shops (quán cà phê) are social institutions. Vietnamese will sit for hours over a single glass, watching the world go by.
