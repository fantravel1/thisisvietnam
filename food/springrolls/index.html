The Rolls

Two Classics

Vietnamese spring rolls come in two iconic forms: the fresh, translucent goi cuon and the crispy, golden cha gio. Both are essential Vietnamese eating experiences.

Goi cuon (fresh rolls) showcase Vietnam's love of fresh herbsâ€”rice paper wrapped around shrimp, pork, vermicelli, and a garden of greens. Cha gio (fried rolls) are the crispy counterpart, stuffed with minced pork and often served wrapped in lettuce.

Roll Varieties

Goi Cuon

Fresh rolls with shrimp, pork, herbs, and vermicelli. Served with peanut hoisin dipping sauce.

Cha Gio

Crispy fried rolls with pork, taro, and glass noodles. Dipped in nuoc cham fish sauce.

Crispy

Bo Bia

Saigon street snack with Chinese sausage, jicama, egg, and dried shrimp in rice paper.
