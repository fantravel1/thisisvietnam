BanhMi

A Symphony in a Sandwich

The Legend

French Meets Vietnamese

When the French brought baguettes to Vietnam, the Vietnamese did what they do best—made it their own. The result is banh mi: arguably the world's greatest sandwich.

The bread is lighter and crispier than French baguettes, made with rice flour. Inside: pate, mayonnaise, various meats (from char siu to cold cuts), pickled daikon and carrots, cucumber, cilantro, jalapeños, and a splash of soy sauce.

Anthony Bourdain called Banh Mi Phuong in Hoi An "a symphony in a sandwich." At $1-2 per sandwich, it's also the best value meal in the world.
