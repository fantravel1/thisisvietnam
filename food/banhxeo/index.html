Vietnamese Classic

BanhXeo

The Sound of Sizzling Happiness

The Dish

Crispy Golden Crepes

"Xèo" is the sound the batter makes when it hits the hot pan—a sizzle that promises crispy, golden happiness. Banh xeo is Vietnam's answer to the crepe, but with rice flour, coconut milk, and turmeric creating something uniquely Vietnamese.

The crispy shell shatters as you bite through to the savory filling of shrimp, pork, and crunchy bean sprouts. But you don't eat it alone—wrap pieces in rice paper or lettuce with herbs, then dip in nuoc cham. The combination of textures and flavors is addictive.

Every region has its version. Southern banh xeo are large, crispy, and coconut-rich. Central versions are smaller and thicker. Hue's banh khoai uses a different batter entirely. All are delicious.

Regional Styles

Banh Xeo Variations

Southern Style

Saigon Banh Xeo

Large, thin, extra crispy with coconut milk batter. Bright yellow color from turmeric. Wrapped in rice paper with herbs.

Central Style

Banh Xeo Mien Trung

Smaller, thicker, and chewier. Often includes quail eggs and green onions. Dipped in peanut sauce variation.

Hue Style

Banh Khoai

Hue's cousin to banh xeo—smaller, folded like an omelet, served with rich peanut-liver dipping sauce.

How to Eat

The Ritual

1. Break It

Tear off a piece of the crispy crepe with your chopsticks or hands.

2. Wrap It

Place on lettuce or rice paper, add herbs (mint, perilla, basil) and pickled vegetables.

3. Roll It

Roll into a tight bundle that captures all the elements.

4. Dip It

Submerge in nuoc cham—the sweet-sour-savory dipping sauce that brings everything together.
