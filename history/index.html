4,000 Years

A History of Resistance

Vietnam has never been easy to conquer. Chinese, Mongol, French, Japanese, American—all tried. All eventually left. This is the story of a nation that refuses to disappear.

Vietnamese history is not a sequence of events—it is a continuous act of survival. Every era brought a new threat, and every era produced Vietnamese who refused to surrender their identity. Understanding this history is understanding why Vietnam is the way it is.

Heroes

Figures Who Shaped Vietnam

From warrior queens to revolutionary leaders—the people Vietnamese remember.

40 CE

The Trưng Sisters

Two sisters who led Vietnam's first major rebellion against Chinese rule. They became queens, ruled for three years, and chose death over surrender. National heroines still honored today.

1428

Emperor Lê Lợi

The peasant leader who defeated the Ming Chinese occupation after ten years of guerrilla warfare. Founded the Lê Dynasty and became the model for Vietnamese resistance.

1890-1969

Hồ Chí Minh

"Uncle Ho"—revolutionary, poet, founding father. The man who declared Vietnamese independence, defeated the French, and became the symbol of Vietnamese nationalism worldwide.

Why This History Matters

You cannot understand modern Vietnam without understanding its history. The Vietnamese obsession with education comes from Confucian tradition. The suspicion of foreign powers comes from a thousand years of Chinese rule and a century of European colonization. The resilience comes from surviving every invasion. The complexity of Vietnamese-American relations comes from a war that killed millions.

Vietnamese history is taught differently in Vietnam than in the West. What Americans call "the Vietnam War," Vietnamese call "the American War"—one chapter in a much longer story of resistance. The perspectives don't align, and that's important to understand.

This site presents Vietnamese history with respect for multiple truths. We use Vietnamese sources alongside Western ones. We center Vietnamese voices. We acknowledge that history is always contested, and that the victors don't always get to write the final version.

History Questions

How old is Vietnamese civilization? +

Vietnamese civilization traces back approximately 4,000 years to the legendary Hùng Kings and the Văn Lang kingdom. Archaeological evidence confirms sophisticated Bronze Age culture (Đông Sơn) dating to around 1000 BCE. Continuous recorded history begins around 200 BCE, making Vietnam one of the oldest continuous civilizations in Southeast Asia.

How long was Vietnam under Chinese rule? +

Vietnam was under direct Chinese rule for approximately 1,000 years (111 BCE to 938 CE), though this period included numerous rebellions and brief periods of independence. This era profoundly influenced Vietnamese culture, language, and political systems, while also forging the fierce independence that defines Vietnamese national identity.

Why do Vietnamese and Americans have different names for the war? +

Americans call it "the Vietnam War" because Vietnam was where Americans fought. Vietnamese call it "the American War" (Chiến tranh chống Mỹ) because America was the enemy they fought. This naming reflects a fundamental difference in perspective: for Americans, it was one war in a foreign land; for Vietnamese, it was one chapter in centuries of fighting foreign powers on their own soil.
