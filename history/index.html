A History of
Resistance

Vietnam has never been easy to conquer. Chinese, Mongol, French, Japanese, American—all tried. All eventually left. This is the story of a nation that refuses to disappear.

Vietnamese history is not a sequence of events—it is a continuous act of survival.

Every era brought a new threat, and every era produced Vietnamese who refused to surrender their identity. Understanding this history is understanding why Vietnam is the way it is.

Context

Why This History Matters

You cannot understand modern Vietnam without understanding its history. The Vietnamese obsession with education comes from Confucian tradition. The suspicion of foreign powers comes from a thousand years of Chinese rule and a century of European colonization.

Vietnamese history is taught differently in Vietnam than in the West. What Americans call "the Vietnam War," Vietnamese call "the American War"—one chapter in a much longer story of resistance.

This site presents Vietnamese history with respect for multiple truths. We use Vietnamese sources alongside Western ones. We center Vietnamese voices. We acknowledge that history is always contested.

History Questions

How old is Vietnamese civilization?

Vietnamese civilization traces back approximately 4,000 years to the legendary Hùng Kings and the Văn Lang kingdom. Archaeological evidence confirms sophisticated Bronze Age culture (Đông Sơn) dating to around 1000 BCE. Continuous recorded history begins around 200 BCE, making Vietnam one of the oldest continuous civilizations in Southeast Asia.

How long was Vietnam under Chinese rule?

Vietnam was under direct Chinese rule for approximately 1,000 years (111 BCE to 938 CE), though this period included numerous rebellions and brief periods of independence. This era profoundly influenced Vietnamese culture, language, and political systems, while also forging the fierce independence that defines Vietnamese national identity.

Why do Vietnamese and Americans have different names for the war?

Americans call it "the Vietnam War" because Vietnam was where Americans fought. Vietnamese call it "the American War" (Chiến tranh chống Mỹ) because America was the enemy they fought. This naming reflects a fundamental difference in perspective: for Americans, it was one war in a foreign land; for Vietnamese, it was one chapter in centuries of fighting foreign powers on their own soil.

What was Đổi Mới and why does it matter?

Đổi Mới ("Renovation") was a series of economic reforms launched in 1986 that transformed Vietnam from a centrally planned economy to a "socialist-oriented market economy." These reforms opened Vietnam to foreign investment, privatized many industries, and sparked decades of rapid economic growth. Đổi Mới explains modern Vietnam's paradox: communist government with capitalist economy.

Who were the Trưng Sisters and why are they important?

Trưng Trắc and Trưng Nhị were two sisters who led Vietnam's first major uprising against Chinese rule in 40 CE. After a Chinese commander killed Trưng Trắc's husband, the sisters raised an army, expelled the Chinese, and ruled as queens for three years. When the Chinese returned with a larger army, they chose suicide over surrender. They remain Vietnam's most revered national heroines, celebrated every year on the anniversary of their rebellion.
