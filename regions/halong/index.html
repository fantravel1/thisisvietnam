The Legend

Bay of Descending Dragons

According to legend, a family of dragons was sent by the Jade Emperor to help Vietnam defend against invaders. The dragons spat jewels and jade, which transformed into the thousands of islands and islets that now dot the bay, forming a natural fortress.

Today, Ha Long Bay stands as one of Earth's most spectacular natural wonders—nearly 2,000 limestone karsts and islands rising dramatically from emerald waters, their surfaces weathered into fantastic shapes over 500 million years.

This UNESCO World Heritage Site draws millions of visitors who come to cruise among the towering pillars, explore hidden caves, kayak through floating villages, and witness sunrises that seem to set the water on fire.

Choosing a Cruise

Budget ($50-100)

Basic boats, shared cabins, standard routes. Fine for the experience, less luxurious.

Mid-Range ($150-250)

Comfortable private cabins, good food, varied activities. Best value for most travelers.

Luxury ($400+)

Boutique boats, gourmet dining, spa services, exclusive routes. Worth it for special occasions.

Activities

Things to Do

Kayaking

Paddle through limestone arches, explore hidden lagoons, and glide past floating villages inaccessible to larger boats.

Sunrise Tai Chi

Join the morning tai chi session on the sundeck as the sun rises over the karsts—a magical way to start the day.

Cave Exploration

Walk through illuminated chambers with stalactites and stalagmites at Sung Sot (Surprise Cave), Thien Cung, or Dau Go caves.

Swimming

Take a dip in the warm emerald waters from designated swimming spots or secluded beaches on uninhabited islands.

Cooking Classes

Learn to make Vietnamese spring rolls, prepare local dishes with the chef, and feast on your creations.

Floating Villages

Visit communities who have lived on the water for generations, rowing between floating homes, schools, and fish farms.

Must See

Iconic Spots

Sung Sot Cave

"Surprise Cave" is Ha Long's largest and most impressive grotto—two enormous chambers with surreal rock formations and panoramic bay views.

Ti Top Island

Climb 400 steps to the summit for the bay's most famous viewpoint, then cool off on the crescent beach below.

Fighting Cocks Islet

The bay's most photographed formation—two karsts leaning toward each other like roosters about to fight. Symbol of Ha Long.

Bai Tu Long Bay

Less touristed extension of Ha Long with equally stunning scenery. Choose cruises here for a more peaceful experience.

Alternative Base

Cat Ba Island

The adventure traveler's gateway to Ha Long

For a different Ha Long experience, base yourself on Cat Ba—the bay's largest island and home to a national park, hidden beaches, and a fraction of the tourist crowds.

Lan Ha Bay

Cat Ba's "own" bay with 400 islets—less crowded, equally beautiful, and perfect for kayaking

Cat Ba National Park

Trek through jungle to stunning viewpoints and spot the endangered Cat Ba langur

Rock Climbing

World-class deep water soloing on limestone cliffs—climb and splash into the bay

Plan Your Visit

Practical Information

Best Time to Visit

October-April for clearest skies. March-May is shoulder season with good weather. Summer (Jun-Aug) can be hazy but warm. Avoid typhoon season (Jul-Sep).

Getting There

3-4 hours from Hanoi by car/bus. Most cruises include pickup. The new expressway has cut travel time significantly. Some cruises depart from Hai Phong.

Booking Tips

Book direct with cruise companies for best prices. Avoid ultra-budget tours. Read recent reviews carefully. Book at least 2 weeks ahead in peak season.

What to Pack

Layers (boats can be cool), swimwear, reef-safe sunscreen, motion sickness meds if prone, waterproof bag for kayaking, camera, binoculars.
