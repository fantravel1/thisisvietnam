The Region

Vietnam's Rice Bowl

The Mekong Delta—known locally as the "Nine Dragon River Delta"—is where the mighty Mekong River fragments into nine branches before emptying into the South China Sea.

This vast, flat expanse of emerald green rice paddies, coconut palms, and fruit orchards produces half of Vietnam's rice and much of its tropical fruit. Life here revolves around water: houses float, markets float, and boats replace roads.

Beyond agriculture, the delta is home to 18 million people living a way of life that has remained largely unchanged for centuries—a world of stilted villages, Buddhist pagodas, and the quiet rhythm of river life.

Must Experience

Floating Markets

Commerce on water, unchanged for centuries

Cai Rang Market

The largest floating market in the delta, starting before dawn when hundreds of boats loaded with produce converge. Each boat displays its wares on tall poles—pineapples, watermelons, cabbages—creating a floating rainbow of commerce.

Best: 5-7 AM Can Tho

Phong Dien Market

Smaller and more authentic than Cai Rang, this market offers a more intimate glimpse into daily delta life. Fewer tourists mean more genuine interactions with local traders who've worked these waters for generations.

Best: 6-8 AM 20km from Can Tho

Cai Be Market

Once the delta's busiest market, now more of a wholesale hub but still fascinating. Combine with visits to nearby fruit orchards and traditional candy workshops on the surrounding islands.

Best: 5-9 AM Tien Giang Province

Delta Destinations

Can Tho

The delta's largest city and gateway. Base yourself here for floating markets, the beautiful waterfront Ninh Kieu Quay, and excellent riverside dining.

Gateway City

Ben Tre

"Coconut Kingdom" of Vietnam. Row through palm-shaded canals, visit coconut candy workshops, and sample coconut-everything—wine, candy, oil, and crafts.

Coconut Paradise

Chau Doc

Border town where Vietnamese, Khmer, and Cham cultures blend. Visit floating fish farms, climb Sam Mountain, and continue to Cambodia via boat.

Cultural Crossroads

My Tho

Closest delta town to Saigon—perfect for day trips. Explore the four islands (Dragon, Unicorn, Phoenix, Tortoise) and Vinh Trang Pagoda.

Day Trip Favorite
Things to Do

Delta Experiences

01

Sampan Boat Rides

Glide through narrow canals shaded by water coconut palms on traditional wooden sampans. The most peaceful way to experience the delta's hidden waterways.

02

Homestays

Spend a night in a stilted house with a local family. Wake to roosters, help with fishing or farming, and feast on home-cooked delta cuisine.

03

Fruit Orchard Visits

Tour tropical orchards bursting with mangoes, dragon fruit, longan, rambutan, and durian. Eat your fill straight from the trees.

04

Cycling the Backroads

Rent a bike and pedal along narrow paths between rice paddies, crossing monkey bridges and stopping at village markets.

05

Traditional Craft Workshops

Learn to make coconut candy, rice paper, traditional cakes, or weave baskets—skills passed down through generations.

Plan Your Visit

Practical Information

Best Time to Visit

December to May (dry season). Floating markets operate year-round but are busiest before Tet. Avoid Sep-Nov when flooding can be severe.

Getting There

My Tho: 1.5 hours from Saigon. Can Tho: 3-4 hours by bus/car, or 45 minutes by flight. Speedboats connect many delta towns.

How Long to Stay

Day trips from Saigon are common but rushed. 2-3 days allows for floating markets, homestays, and proper delta immersion.

Budget

The delta is very affordable. Homestays from $15/night, boat tours $10-30, meals $2-5. Tours from Saigon start around $25 for day trips.
