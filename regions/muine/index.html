The Region

Desert by the Sea

Mui Ne is Vietnam's unlikely desert—a coastal strip where red and white sand dunes meet palm-fringed beaches. Strong, consistent winds have made it Asia's top kitesurfing destination, while the surreal landscapes draw photographers and adventurers.

Once a quiet fishing village, Mui Ne transformed into a resort strip in the 2000s. Despite development, it retains its natural wonders and authentic fishing culture. The early morning fish market remains one of Vietnam's most photogenic scenes.

The microclimate here is unique in Vietnam—semi-arid with less rainfall than anywhere else in the country. This means reliable sunshine and those famous dune-sculpting winds year-round.

Explore

Things to Do

Sunrise/Sunset

Red Sand Dunes

Orange-red dunes close to town. Best at sunset when colors deepen. Rent a plastic sled and slide down the slopes.

Early Morning

White Sand Dunes

Larger, more dramatic Sahara-like dunes. ATV rides available. Visit at dawn before the heat and crowds.

November-April

Kitesurfing

Consistent 15-25 knot winds make this Vietnam's premier spot. Multiple schools offer lessons for all levels.

More Activities

Beyond the Dunes

Fairy Stream

Wade through this ankle-deep stream between red and white sandstone formations. Surreal mini-canyon landscape.

Fishing Village

Watch the morning catch come in. Hundreds of colorful round basket boats bob in the harbor at dawn.

Po Shanu Cham Towers

8th-century Hindu temple ruins overlooking the coast. Early morning or sunset for best light.

Seafood Feast

Fresh catch at beachfront restaurants. Point at what you want, choose your cooking style.

Golf

Two quality courses including the Nick Faldo-designed Ocean Dunes with stunning coastal holes.

Spa & Wellness

Resort spas offering everything from Vietnamese massage to mud baths and hot springs.

Plan Your Visit

Practical Info

Getting There

4 hours from Saigon by bus or car. Train to Phan Thiet station (30 min taxi). Some resorts offer transfers.

Best Time

November-March for kitesurfing (strongest winds). Year-round for dunes and beach. Avoid September-October rains.

Where to Stay

Resorts line the beach road. Budget to luxury options. Choose east end for quieter beaches, west for kitesurfing.

How Long

2-3 nights to cover main sights. Longer if learning to kitesurf or seeking serious beach time.
