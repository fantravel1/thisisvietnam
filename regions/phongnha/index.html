Ancient Wonder

Earth's Greatest Caves

Hidden in the jungles of central Vietnam lies an underground world that rewrites superlatives. Here you'll find the world's largest cave passage (Son Doong), one of the longest underground rivers, and a karst landscape 400 million years in the making.

Phong Nha-Ke Bang National Park protects one of Earth's most significant karst regions. Over 300 caves honeycomb the ancient limestone mountains, many still unexplored, their formations sculpted by water over hundreds of millions of years.

This isn't just geology—it's a lost world. Son Doong contains its own weather system, jungle, and river. Paradise Cave stretches 31 kilometers into the darkness. The Phong Nha cave system still guards secrets from the Vietnam War, when it served as a supply depot and hospital along the Ho Chi Minh Trail.

Underground Adventures

The Caves

From easy walks to extreme expeditions

Half Day

Paradise Cave

The most accessible of Phong Nha's great caves. Walk 1km into a cathedral of stalactites on elevated boardwalks, or extend to 7km with a guide.

From $10
Half Day

Dark Cave

Zip-line across the river, swim into darkness, and wade through waist-deep mud. Pure adventure caving that's fun for all fitness levels.

From $45
4 Days

Son Doong

The ultimate cave expedition. Trek through jungle, camp inside the world's largest cave passage, witness underground jungles lit by skylights.

From $3,000

Booking Son Doong

Only 1,000 permits are issued annually for Son Doong. Oxalis is the sole licensed operator. Book 6-12 months in advance. The trek requires good fitness and costs around $3,000 USD for the 4-day expedition. Alternatives like Hang En offer similar experiences at lower cost.

Beyond the Caves

Things to Do

Botanical Garden

Hike jungle trails to natural pools and small caves in the lush Phong Nha Botanical Garden. Perfect for a cooler morning.

Kayaking

Paddle the Song River through stunning karst scenery. Combine with Phong Nha Cave for a boat-and-kayak adventure.

Ho Chi Minh Trail

Explore the legendary wartime supply route. Visit abandoned tank depots, tunnels, and learn the incredible history.

Phong Nha Village

Stay in this charming village base. Rent a motorbike, visit local farms, and enjoy the countryside hospitality.

Night Caving

Experience caves after dark on specialized night tours. Watch for bats, nocturnal creatures, and see formations in a new light.

Countryside Tours

Join local families for farmstays, buffalo rides, and cooking classes. Experience authentic rural Vietnam.

Plan Your Visit

Practical Information

Best Time to Visit

February-August for dry season. September-January sees flooding that closes some caves. April-May offers best weather before summer heat.

Getting There

Fly to Dong Hoi airport (15 min drive). Train to Dong Hoi station (30 min). Or bus from Hanoi (10 hrs) / Hue (4 hrs). Most accommodations offer transfers.

How Long to Stay

Minimum 2 nights to see Paradise and Dark caves. 3-4 nights to add trekking and the Phong Nha cave. Longer for serious expeditions.

What to Pack

Quick-dry clothes, water shoes, headlamp, waterproof bag, reef-safe sunscreen, insect repellent. Caves are cool so bring a light layer.
