Questions

Common questions answered with the depth they deserve.

What is Vietnam really like?

Vietnam is a nation of profound depth—where ancient traditions meet modern energy, where war trauma coexists with remarkable resilience, and where food is not just sustenance but memory, identity, and love. It's chaotic city streets at 6 AM, peaceful rice paddies, fierce family bonds, and a culture that has absorbed foreign influences while remaining distinctly Vietnamese.

What makes Vietnamese culture unique?

Vietnamese culture is shaped by 4,000 years of history, Confucian values blended with Buddhist and animist traditions, a fierce independence that resisted Chinese, French, and American influence, and regional identities that vary dramatically from north to south. Family is the center of life, food is sacred, and resilience is woven into the national character.

How important is food in Vietnamese culture?

Food in Vietnam is identity itself. The greeting "Ăn cơm chưa?"—Have you eaten rice yet?—reveals how central food is to daily life. Each dish tells a story: phở emerged from colonial influence meeting northern traditions, bánh mì represents cultural fusion, and regional specialties carry centuries of local knowledge.

What should I know before visiting Vietnam?

Vietnam rewards curiosity and humility. Learn a few Vietnamese phrases—locals deeply appreciate the effort. Understand that "the Vietnam War" is called "the American War" here. Be prepared for sensory overload: the traffic, the heat, the noise are part of the experience. And eat everything—the best food is often on tiny plastic stools on the sidewalk.
