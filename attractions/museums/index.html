VietnamMuseums

Understanding the Past, Experiencing the Present

The Collections

Stories in Every Hall

Vietnam's museums don't shy away from difficult history. From the devastation of war to colonial oppression, from ethnic diversity to ancient civilizations, these institutions tell Vietnam's story with power and honesty.

Some will leave you shaken, others amazed. All will deepen your understanding of this complex, resilient nation. Allow time for reflection—these aren't places to rush through.

Must Visit

Essential Museums

Ho Chi Minh City

War Remnants Museum

Powerful, often disturbing exhibition on the Vietnam War from the Vietnamese perspective. Military hardware, Agent Orange effects, war photography.

Hanoi

Hoa Lo Prison

"Hanoi Hilton" to American POWs, but first built by French to hold Vietnamese revolutionaries. Two stories of imprisonment and resistance.

Hanoi

Ethnology Museum

Vietnam's 54 ethnic groups brought to life through traditional houses, costumes, tools, and ceremonies. Excellent outdoor exhibits.

Near Saigon

Cu Chi Tunnels

Crawl through the underground network where Viet Cong fighters lived and fought. Claustrophobic but unforgettable experience.

Da Nang

Museum of Cham Sculpture

World's largest collection of Cham art—Hindu deities, temple friezes, and sculptures from a lost civilization.

Hue

Imperial City Museums

Within the Citadel, multiple museums showcase royal artifacts, the Nguyen Dynasty, and Hue's UNESCO heritage.

Also Worth Visiting

More Museums

Fine Arts Museums

Hanoi and Saigon both have excellent collections of Vietnamese lacquerware, silk paintings, and contemporary art.

Women's Museum

Hanoi's tribute to Vietnamese women's role in history, culture, and family life. Beautifully curated.

Ho Chi Minh Museums

Several across the country dedicated to "Uncle Ho"—his life, ideology, and the revolution.

Independence Palace

Saigon's former presidential palace, frozen in time from 1975. Where the Vietnam War ended.
