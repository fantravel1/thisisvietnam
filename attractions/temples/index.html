Sacred Spaces Across the Land

Spiritual Vietnam

Where Faith Meets Art

Vietnam's temples tell the story of the nation's spiritual journey—from ancient Hindu Cham towers to Chinese Mahayana pagodas, from Cao Dai's psychedelic cathedral to simple village shrines honoring ancestors.

Buddhism arrived from China and India, blending with indigenous beliefs. The result is a uniquely Vietnamese spiritual landscape where Buddhist, Taoist, and Confucian elements coexist, and where ancestor worship underlies everything.

Must Visit

Iconic Temples

Near Hanoi

Perfume Pagoda

Boat ride through karst scenery, then cable car or hike to this sacred cave temple. Vietnam's most important Buddhist pilgrimage site.

Hanoi

One Pillar Pagoda

Built in 1049 to resemble a lotus flower rising from water. Small but iconic symbol of Hanoi.

Central Vietnam

My Son Sanctuary

UNESCO Cham temple ruins dating to 4th century. Vietnam's most important archaeological site.

Ninh Binh

Bai Dinh Pagoda

Vietnam's largest pagoda complex with 500 arhat statues and Southeast Asia's largest bronze Buddha.

Tay Ninh

Cao Dai Holy See

Headquarters of Vietnam's homegrown religion. Psychedelic architecture blending East and West for noon ceremonies.

Hue

Thien Mu Pagoda

Seven-story pagoda on the Perfume River. Houses the car that carried a monk to his self-immolation in 1963.

Temple Etiquette

How to Visit

Dress Modestly

Cover shoulders and knees. Long pants/skirts preferred. Some sites provide cover-ups at entry.

Remove Shoes

Take off footwear before entering temple buildings. Leave at the entrance or carry in a bag.

Be Quiet

Lower your voice inside temple grounds. Many people are praying and meditating.

Ask Before Photos

Photography often permitted outdoors but restricted inside. Never photograph people praying without permission.

Respect Altars

Don't touch offerings, Buddha statues, or altar items. Walk around clockwise if circumambulating.

Light Incense

Visitors often welcome to light incense. Three sticks is traditional—one for each of the Three Jewels.
