Pristine Shores from North to South

The Coast

Endless Shoreline

Vietnam's S-shaped coastline stretches over 3,000 kilometers, offering everything from developed resort beaches to deserted islands, from kite-surfing hotspots to traditional fishing villages.

The diversity is remarkable: white sand and turquoise water in Phu Quoc, dramatic boulders in Quy Nhon, golden dunes in Mui Ne, jungle-backed coves in Con Dao. Every beach has its own character.

Top Picks

Best Beaches

Phu Quoc Island

Vietnam's largest island with powdery white sand, sunset beaches, and snorkeling. Long Beach and Sao Beach are standouts.

Remote Paradise

Con Dao Islands

Pristine, uncrowded archipelago with turtle nesting beaches, excellent diving, and powerful history. Vietnam's best-kept secret.

Quy Nhon

By Season

When to Visit

Northern Beaches

May-September for Cat Ba and Ha Long area. Winter months cold and gray.

Central Coast

February-August for Da Nang, Hoi An, Quy Nhon. Typhoon risk September-December.

Southern Beaches

November-April for Nha Trang, Phu Quoc. Dry season with calm seas.

Year-Round

Phu Quoc and Con Dao have good weather most of the year except September-October monsoon.
